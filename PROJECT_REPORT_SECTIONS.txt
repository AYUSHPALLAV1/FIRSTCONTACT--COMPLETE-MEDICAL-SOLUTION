FIRSTCONTACT – REPORT CORE SECTIONS
================================================================================

1. ABSTRACT
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
FirstContact is an integrated digital healthcare platform that acts as a
patient’s first point of contact when they experience symptoms. The system
collects basic information, vital signs, symptoms, family history, and lifestyle
factors, and processes them through a Random Forest–based machine learning
engine to generate a preliminary diagnosis with confidence scores. Based on the
predicted conditions, the platform recommends appropriate specialists, enables
online consultation booking, helps users discover nearby hospitals, and
analyzes health insurance policies to estimate coverage. By combining AI-driven
triage, doctor discovery, and insurance analysis in a single workflow,
FirstContact reduces the time between symptom onset and professional care,
especially for users who may not have immediate access to a doctor.


2. INTRODUCTION
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Healthcare systems often struggle at the very first step of the care journey:
when a patient decides whether their symptoms are serious and whom they should
consult. Many people rely on web searches or generic symptom checkers, which
can be inaccurate, anxiety-inducing, and disconnected from the next steps such
as booking a doctor or checking insurance coverage. At the same time, hospitals
and clinics face increasing load on outpatient departments, with many visits
that could have been routed more efficiently if a structured triage process was
available at home.

FirstContact addresses this gap by providing an end-to-end digital “front
door” for healthcare. The web-based system guides the user through a structured
multi-step form that captures demographics, vitals, symptoms, family history,
and lifestyle data. This information is sent to a Python-based ML engine that
uses an ensemble model (Random Forest Classifier) to predict the top probable
conditions. The platform then closes the loop by recommending relevant
specialists, enabling appointment scheduling, showing nearby hospitals on an
interactive map, and analyzing uploaded insurance policies. The result is a
single platform that connects clinical insight, logistics, and financial
planning.


3. OBJECTIVES
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
- To design and implement an AI-driven preliminary diagnosis engine using a
  Random Forest Classifier trained on synthetic clinical data.
- To capture structured patient information including vitals, symptoms, family
  history, and lifestyle factors via an intuitive multi-step web interface.
- To provide automated specialist recommendations and online consultation
  booking aligned with the predicted conditions.
- To integrate hospital discovery using mapping services so that users can
  locate nearby facilities suited to their needs.
- To analyze digital insurance policy documents and estimate coverage for the
  predicted conditions, reducing financial uncertainty.
- To create a secure, scalable, and user-friendly system that can serve as the
  first digital touchpoint in a patient’s healthcare journey.


4. SCOPE
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
- The system focuses on preliminary, non-diagnostic triage based on user-
  provided data; it does not replace professional medical judgment.
- The current implementation targets common lifestyle and chronic conditions
  (for example, hypertension, diabetes risk, cardiovascular issues, and common
  infections) where vitals and simple symptoms provide strong signals.
- The platform supports individual users accessing the web application on
  desktop or mobile devices; extensions to hospital kiosks or mobile apps are
  feasible future enhancements.
- Appointment booking covers listing doctors, viewing their specialization and
  availability, and creating appointment entries; deeper integration with
  hospital EMR systems is outside the current scope.
- Insurance analysis is limited to reading policy text, matching diseases with
  coverage keywords, and presenting high-level coverage indications rather than
  legally binding decisions.


5. LITERATURE REVIEW (SUMMARY)
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Existing work in AI-assisted healthcare can be grouped into three areas:

- Symptom checkers and online triage:
  Several systems use decision trees or rule-based logic to suggest possible
  conditions based on symptom inputs. While interpretable, they often ignore
  vitals and blood-test data, and they do not connect users to concrete next
  steps such as booking a doctor.

- Machine learning for disease prediction:
  Research has shown that ensemble methods such as Random Forests perform well
  for predicting chronic diseases like diabetes, heart disease, and kidney
  disorders using mixed numerical and categorical features. These models are
  robust to noise, handle missing values through averaging, and provide
  probability scores that are useful for triage rather than binary classification.

- Digital health platforms and telemedicine:
  Teleconsultation systems and hospital portals exist for booking appointments
  and managing records. However, most of them assume that the user already knows
  which specialist to see and do not include AI-based triage linked to insurance
  analysis.

FirstContact combines insights from these three lines of work: using ensemble
ML for probabilistic triage, embedding it inside a practical teleconsultation
workflow, and adding an insurance-coverage layer that most academic systems
do not address.


6. ARCHITECTURAL DIAGRAM (TEXTUAL DESCRIPTION)
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
The high-level architecture can be described as follows:

- Client Layer:
  A responsive web frontend (HTML, CSS, JavaScript) that renders the home page,
  Medidose form, insurance page, and consultation page. It communicates with
  the backend using REST APIs and displays results such as predicted diseases,
  doctor lists, and insurance coverage summaries.

- Application Layer:
  A Node.js/Express server that manages user authentication, routes incoming
  requests, and orchestrates interactions between the frontend, machine learning
  engine, and database. It exposes endpoints for prediction, doctor management,
  appointments, and insurance analysis.

- ML Engine Layer:
  A Python module invoked as a child process by the Node.js backend. It loads
  a trained Random Forest model, preprocesses incoming data (BMI calculation,
  vitals normalization, one-hot encoding of symptoms/history/lifestyle), and
  returns the top probable conditions with confidence scores and meta-data.

- Data Layer:
  A MongoDB database storing user profiles, doctor details, appointments, and
  saved predictions. It supports querying by specialization, location, and user
  ID to enable personalized experiences.

- Integration Layer:
  External services such as Google Maps for hospital location and Stripe (or a
  similar gateway) for payments can be plugged in to complete the workflow.

In a graphical architectural diagram, these components would be represented as
separate blocks connected by arrows indicating data flow between browser,
backend, ML engine, database, and external APIs.


7. FLOW DIAGRAM (TEXTUAL DESCRIPTION)
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
The operational flow of FirstContact can be described step by step:

1. The user visits the home page and logs in or registers.
2. The user navigates to the Medidose page and fills out the multi-step form:
   basic details, vitals, symptoms, family history, and lifestyle.
3. On submission, the frontend sends the collected data as JSON to the backend
   prediction endpoint.
4. The Node.js server validates the request and spawns the Python ML engine,
   passing the data to the Random Forest model.
5. The ML engine preprocesses the data, runs inference, and returns the top
   predicted conditions with confidence scores and recommended specialist types.
6. The backend forwards this result to the frontend, which displays it to the
   user along with severity/urgency information.
7. The user can choose to:
   - Book an appointment with a recommended specialist,
   - View nearby hospitals on the consultation page, or
   - Upload/view an insurance policy on the insurance page.
8. Appointment details are stored in MongoDB; insurance text is analyzed to
   highlight whether the predicted conditions are likely to be covered.
9. The user receives confirmation of the next steps (consultation details,
   hospital directions, or insurance coverage summary).

In a standard flowchart, these steps would be represented using start/end
terminals, process blocks for data collection and prediction, decision blocks
for user choices, and arrows showing the progression from input to actions.


8. LIST OF MODULES
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
- User Authentication and Home Module:
  Handles registration, login, session management, and navigation between the
  main pages of the application.

- Medidose (AI Triage) Module:
  Multi-step form for collecting patient data and invoking the ML engine to
  generate preliminary disease predictions with explanations.

- Consultation and Doctor Management Module:
  Manages doctor profiles, displays available specialists, and allows users to
  book appointments and view upcoming consultations.

- Hospital Discovery Module:
  Integrates with mapping services to show nearby hospitals and clinics relevant
  to the predicted conditions or chosen specialist.

- Insurance Analysis Module:
  Allows users to input or upload insurance policy details and runs text-based
  analysis to estimate coverage for the predicted diseases.

- Payment and Confirmation Module (optional/extendable):
  Supports payment initiation for consultations and records confirmations for
  user reference.

- Administration and Data Management Module:
  Provides backend scripts and interfaces for seeding doctor data, managing
  records, and monitoring system health.
